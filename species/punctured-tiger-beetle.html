Punctured Tiger Beetle (photo: your name / date or iNaturalist observer)

Identification

  • Small-medium (10–13 mm), dark bronze to black with punctured (pitted) elytra and variable white marks.
  • Body duller than others; legs dark.
  • Active in summer; short flights.

Habitat in New Brunswick

Open fields, sandy paths, gravel areas, and disturbed ground. Prefers sunny, bare soil.

Flight Season in NB

May to September (summer peak June-August).

Distribution & Notes

Scattered across NB; locally common in open habitats. Summer-active species.
