Twelve-spotted Tiger Beetle (photo: your name / date or iNaturalist observer)

Identification

  • Medium-sized (12–15 mm), bronze to olive-green with usually 12 white spots/marks on elytra (may fuse or reduce).
  • White labrum; often iridescent green underside.
  • Fast runner and flyer on moist sandy/muddy soils.
  • Distinguish from Bronzed Tiger Beetle by more spots and less coppery tone.

Habitat in New Brunswick

Widespread on riverbanks, sandbars, trails, and disturbed moist areas. Common across the province, especially near water.

Flight Season in NB

April to October (spring-fall bimodal; peaks May-June and August-September). One of the longer active species.

Distribution & Notes

Widespread and common; good indicator of moist open habitats. No conservation concerns in NB.
