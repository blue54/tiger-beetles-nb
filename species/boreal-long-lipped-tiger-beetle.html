Boreal Long-lipped Tiger Beetle

Cicindela longilabris longilabris

Boreal Long-lipped Tiger Beetle (photo: your name / date or iNaturalist observer)

Distribution map (based on iNaturalist observations)

Identification

  • Large tiger beetle (15–19 mm), dark bronze to black with metallic green reflections.
  • Long labrum (upper lip) extending forward; white maculations variable or reduced.
  • Legs long and slender; fast flier and runner in open, dry habitats.
  • Distinguished from similar species by size, labrum shape, and boreal distribution.

Habitat in New Brunswick

Found in open boreal forests, sandy trails, gravel pits, and dry clearings. Prefers sunny, sparsely vegetated areas; often on paths or bare ground in northern and central NB.

Flight Season in NB

May to September (spring-fall; peaks June–July). Adults active in warm weather on sunny days.

Distribution & Notes

Primarily northern and central New Brunswick in boreal zones. Less common in southern counties. Good indicator of open, dry forest edges. No conservation concerns.
